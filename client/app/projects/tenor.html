Visit Tenor.co

I started working with Tenor in the winter of 2014/2015. While with Tenor, I spent much of my time revamping the web experience from the ground up. From a product and design perspective, I owned the web platform from start to finish.

This included sketching out wireframes and presenting them for feedback. I also designed hi-fidelity mock ups in Sketch and created interactions in Origami/Framer JS. We had no product or engineering managers during my time at Tenor, so I was in constant communication with the engineering team about proper implementation. This allowed me to better grasp what a healthy productive relationship between an engineer and product designer should feel like.

Through my time at Tenor I learned how to go into the code base and jam on the CSS/HTML. I quickly realized that with a small engineering team, they often don't want to be bothered with the finite design details. At first it was overwhelming, but I quickly moved past that and began to take the initiative of cleaning up the CSS/HTMl as a source of pride. It's now one of the favorite parts of my job.
